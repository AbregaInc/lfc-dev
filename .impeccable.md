## Design Context

### Users
LFC is for engineering leads, platform teams, and technically opinionated developers who need to push trusted AI tooling setup across a mixed-tool team. They are usually coordinating Claude Code, Cursor, Codex, desktop MCPs, rules, and shared instructions, and they want rollout to feel operational rather than improvised.

### Brand Personality
Exact, calm, and quietly opinionated. The interface should feel trustworthy, controlled, and sharp enough for technical operators, without drifting into cyberpunk, playful startup energy, or generic dark "AI dashboard" aesthetics.

### Aesthetic Direction
Warm editorial operations software. Prefer light, paper-like surfaces with ink-heavy typography, copper/rust accents, restrained greens for health, and layouts that feel composed rather than ornamental. Avoid neon gradients, glassmorphism, pure black backgrounds, and anything that reads like "future AI platform" by default.

### Design Principles
- Make rollout reliability explicit. Users should see managed, best-effort, and unreliable states without digging.
- Show the file-level mechanics without making the product feel raw or fragile.
- Use typography, spacing, and composition to signal confidence; avoid decorative noise.
- Keep dense operational views readable, breathable, and obviously actionable.
- Favor trustworthy operator tooling over hype-driven AI branding.
